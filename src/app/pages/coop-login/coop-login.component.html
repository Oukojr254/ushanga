The Ushanga Kenya initiative seeks to strengthen the business and production capacity of women and youth as well as improve the competitiveness of bead products in the local,regional and international markets for sustainable livelihoods, this can be achieved through formation of cooperatives where the women join efforts to make the products more efficiently, more so the initiative seeks to protect the intellectual property of the communities practicing beadwork and preserve the bead culture by setting up channels that directly link the women to the buyers and monitoring any illegal activities taking place on behalf of the women.
